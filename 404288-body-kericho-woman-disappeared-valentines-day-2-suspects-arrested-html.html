A 30-year-old Kericho woman who went missing on Valentine's Day has been found dead.

Emmy Chemutai's body was retrieved from a shallow grave at a thicket in Kapsongoi Tea Estate on Thursday, February 25.

The deceased was a worker at James Finlay Tea Company and had been employed by the company since 2004.

Two suspects arrested

Detectives have arrested two suspects connected with her murder and have launched a manhunt for her boyfriend, who is on the run.

Kericho county police boss Silas Gichunge said preliminary investigations showed the two suspects took part in killing Chemutai before burying her body in a shallow makeshift grave.

"We are looking for the deceased's boyfriend who is on the run. We believe that alongside the two suspects, they gang-raped the woman before burying her body at Kapsongoi Tea Estate," Gichunge said at a press conference.

Chemutai's uncle Samuel Tonui noted her legs were tied at the ankle with a sisal rope, indicating she could have been tortured and left for the dead.

Her brother Festus Kipsang said even though the turn of events was painful, their family was relieved to get her body after a long search.

Kipsang said they were not aware their kin was dating someone as she lived alone.
