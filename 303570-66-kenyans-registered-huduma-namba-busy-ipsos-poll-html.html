66% of Kenyans have not registered for Huduma Namba, say the're too busy - Ipsos poll

April 07, 2024
Post

- The recent survey indicated a vast majority of Kenyans were aware of the Huduma Namna registration

- The poll indicated only a partly 34% had registered in the mass biometric system

- Majority of them claimed they lead busy lives hence finding time to register remained a challenge

Even as the government continues to pile pressure on Kenyans to register for the controversial Huduma Namba, it has now emerged that more than half of the citizens have not actually taken the initiative.

Up to 66% of Kenyans are not yet registered to the National Integrated Identity Management System according to a recent poll.

This according to an Ipsos study conducted between Monday, April 15 and Thursday, April 18.

In a survey that saw 1,527 people interviewed across the country, Nairobi and Coastal regions had the largest number of people at 71 % who were yet to register for the mass biometric system.

The Eastern region had the highest number of people who had registered (42%) although an unfortunate trend countrywide was the fact that less than half the population had their details captured electronically.

Led by President Uhuru Kenyatta, the government has for the better part of April 2019 pushed Kenyans to surrender their details to the system which is said to be a portal to an array of government services.

So why are Kenyans still so reluctant to register for the Huduma Namba?

According to the Ipsos’ survey, majority of them are yet to to do it for the simple fact that they are too busy with other commitments.

READ ALSO: Huduma Namba: Govt to switch off phone SIM cards of those who fail to beat registration deadline

About 46% of the respondents cited a “busy schedule” as one of the main reasons they were yet to register, while a further 16% claimed the long queues deterred them from the exercise.

Quite astonishingly, 15% of Kenyans had no idea of the Huduma Namba’s use hence they did not really prioritise the registration.

Out of the 66% of Kenyans yet to register, up to 93% expressed intention to actually take up the exercise.

Meanwhile the government has issued two contradicting statements regarding what is at stake if one fails to secure a Huduma Namba.

On one hand, the Communications Authority of Kenya (CK) warned of the possibility of a network lockdown for individuals who fail to register, meaning they would be unable to operate their SIM cards as they would be blocked.

But State House, in a parallel statement, denounced the warning, saying no such directive had been issued.
