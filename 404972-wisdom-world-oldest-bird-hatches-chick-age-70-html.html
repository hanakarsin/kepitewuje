The world's oldest known bird, Wisdom albatross, has hatched another chick at the age of 70.

Wisdom hatched the new baby in the Midway Atoll national wildlife refuge in a US minor outlying island in the North Pacific.

The Midway Atoll wildlife refuge is home to the largest colony of albatross in the world.

According to the US Fish and Wildlife Service (USFWS), wisdom laid the egg during the last days of November 2020 and hatched the chick on Monday, February 1.

However, the information was kept secret until recently when USFWS made the announcement.

Wisdom is 'married' to Akeakamai, and they are reported to have been together since 2012 when they were discovered and labelled as a couple by scientists.

The bird is said to have outlived its generation and has been finding new mates along the way.

According to biologists, Laysan albatrosses have a life span of between 12-40 years. However, wisdom has exceeded this by far as her age is currently estimated to be at least 70 years.

“At least 70 years old, we believe Wisdom has had other mates. Though albatross mate for life, they may find new partners if necessary — for example, if they outlive their first mate,” said U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service biologist Dr. Beth Flint.

Albatross do not lay eggs as frequently as other birds do. In a year, this species of wild birds may only lay one egg or even fail completely. The incubation period after an albatross lays an egg is typically 65 days.

Albatross parents share incubation duties, and once the chick hatches, they share feeding duties.

For the 70 years she has been alive, Wisdom is estimated to have hatched at least 30–36 chicks.
