Gatundu South Member of Parliament Moses Kuria has thrown jibes at his Rarieda counterpart Otiende Amollo after he was kicked out as the vice chairman and member of the National Assembly's Justice and Legal Affairs Committee (JLAC).

The vocal Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker who doesn't mince his words shared his old post where he had warned Amollo and other Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) members to toe the party line on matters Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) lest they face the wrath of the outfit's leadership.

"To my fellow congregant at St Austin’s Catholic Hon Otiende Amollo...This was July 15, 2020, nine months ago. And now! Next time I call you, come with a pen and a notebook. This is politics not law. Otherwise, you will cry all the way to Kit Mikaye," Kuria sarcastically posted.

Amollo was on Tuesday, May 4, removed from the committee mandated with the implementation report of the Constitution of Kenya (Amendment Bill) 2020 after he expressed concerns over the creation of 70 new constituencies as proposed in BBI.

On Saturday, May 1, the outspoken lawmaker while speaking in Alego Usonga, said it was important to pass BBI and ensure ODM leader Raila Odinga succeeds President Uhuru Kenyatta.

"The first goal is passing the BBI while the second one is ensuring that Raila Odinga becomes the fifth president," Amollo said.

He, however, accused some party members and officials of blocking Raila's presidential bid.

"Unfortunately, these are people who have their own issues and conviction unlike me who have my own primary and fundamental goals. The rest we can deal with," he explained.

Mbadi directs ODM MPs to toe party line on BBI

The ODM party chairman John Mbadi had earlier directed its members in Parliament to endorse the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Bill in its entirety.

Mbadi on Tuesday, April 27 asked members to toe the party line and pass the Constitution of Kenya (Amendment) Bill 2020 without being drawn into making unnecessary amendments.

“It is the party's position that Parliament should pass the BBI Bill as it is, in its entirety. This is, therefore, a rallying call to our members and supporters out there to prepare to vote for the bill once it is presented to them in the referendum,” said Mbadi, who doubles up as the Suba South MP.
