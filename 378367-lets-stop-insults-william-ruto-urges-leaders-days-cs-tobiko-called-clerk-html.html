Let's stop insults: William Ruto urges leaders days after CS Tobiko called him clerk

Deputy President William Ruto has urged leaders to desist from pride and insults a couple of days after Environment Cabinet Secretary Keriako Tobiko said he was only a clerk to President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Ruto said leaders serving in the Jubilee government should focus their energies on improving Kenyans' lives and not non-issues such as insults.

Speaking during a church service in Athi River on Sunday, September 6, the second in command also called on leaders to be humble stating leadership was a blessing that should be used to serve others.

"I ask those who have this opportunity to serve in various positions to stop the pride and insults so that we can reach the levels that President Uhuru Kenyatta and the Jubilee government so as to bring development in the country," he said.

Just a clerk

Ruto's remarks came days after Tobiko, who had been angered by Senator Kipchumba Murkomen's sentiments, said the DP was a clerk.

The CS called on the two leaders to show respect if indeed they needed to be treated the same way.

Tobiko who was irked by Murkomen's lectures said the politician together with Ruto were disrespectful and often insulted the president.

"His boss (Ruto) is a clerk of the president. The deputy president is a clerk, he should be a clerk. I expect them to respect the president the same way I respect him. Respect cuts both ways, it is two-way traffic. So if you don't respect of Kenya, you don't deserve to be respected by anybody," the former director of public prosecutions said.
