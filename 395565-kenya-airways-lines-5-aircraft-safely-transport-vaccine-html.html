Kenya Airways lines up 5 aircraft to safely transport COVID-19 vaccine

July 05, 2024
Post

- Kenya Airways said at least five of its freighters are fitted with temperature control systems for use during transportation of temperature-sensitive vaccines and other pharmaceutical products

- The airline revealed it was going to explore the usage of cooler boxes with dry ice in the transportation of vaccines that need very cold storage

- Already, a cold warehouse has been set up at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA); it will play an integral role in transportation and storage of vaccines

National carrier Kenya Airways (KQ) has revealed it will be dedicating at least five of its planes to hauling of COVID-19 vaccines across the world as more drugmakers receive emergency authorisation for their doses.

The airline's Cargo manager Peter Musola exclusively told TUKO.co.ke among the planes that will be utilised in vaccine transportation include three Boeing 787-8, two Boeing 737 freighters and some passenger planes that allow belly loading.

According to Musola, the five planes are fitted with temperature controls that can support cold storage which is needed by most vaccines for them to function properly once administered.

"Currently, KQ has in plan to use two to three Boeing 787-8 repurposed aircraft and two Boeing 737 freighters alongside all our passenger service aircraft that allow belly-hold loading," Musola said.

For vaccines such as Pfizer which require cold storage of minus 70 degrees celsius, the KQ official said the airline was considering use of cooler boxes with dry ice during transportation.

He nonetheless opined that a vaccine that may be deployed within the continent will most likely be a version that does not require extremely cold storage.

"Our Boeing 787-8 aircraft do have temperature management equipment. However, the vaccines will be loaded into either special aircraft containers (envirotainers) which are then loaded into the aircraft (active containers). Other alternatives are having the vaccines in cool boxes with dry ice. These will be the primary temperature management elements. The aircraft controls come as secondary elements. These are considerations to be done by the pharmaceutical shippers dependent on the destination of vaccines. Important to note is that there are vaccines like from AstraZeneca (2-8 degrees) which can be managed using the second temperature control setting ranges available in our aircraft without using cool boxes with dry ice or envirotainers. In all cases, the KQ infrastructure, both on-air and the ground will heavily support the transport and handling logistics. It could also be a possibility that the vaccines distributed across the Africa continent will be more of the type with lesser extreme temperature conditions," Musola explained.

As separately reported by TUKO.co.ke, KQ has already set up a modern Pharma Cargo facility with temperature controls that is able to hold at least 300 tons of pharmaceutical products that are temperature sensitive.

KQ's move to step up its special cargo holding facility came at a time when dozens of airlines and other logistic companies across the world are in a race to prepare to transport vaccines.

Section of freighters across the globe have fitted their cabins with special cooling equipment in a move to tap into the vaccine freighting market.

The UK is among countries that have already started vaccinating their populations.

Vaccination kicked off on Tuesday, December 8, and a 90-year-old woman was the first to receive the Pfizer jab that was recently approved by Prime Minister Boris Johson-led administration.

Russian authorities started a mass rollout of Sputnik V vaccine on Saturday, December 5, while China, which has at least two approved doses, has sent consignments to Indonesia.

On Thursday, December 10, the US is set to decide the fate of Pfizer dose which has filed for emergency use. Moderna will wait until December 22.

A vaccine made by AstraZeneca and Oxford University is also expected to be granted emergency authorisation soon.

Kenya is still conducting early trials with the AstraZeneca dose but at the same time shopping for a viable vaccine.

Sputnik V and AstraZeneca vaccines can be stored under fridge temperature while Moderna needs to be stored at minus 20 degrees celcius.
