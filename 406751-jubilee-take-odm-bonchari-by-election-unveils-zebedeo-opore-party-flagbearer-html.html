Jubilee to Take on ODM in Bonchari By-Election, Unveils Zebedeo Opore as Party Flagbearer

June 12, 2024
Post

- The Jubilee Party picked former Bonchari MP Zebedeo Opore, who will fight for the seat with ODM and UDA candidates

- The former embattled Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority director-general Pavel Oimeke will fly ODM's flag in Bonchari

- The ruling party also settled on the former Garissa senator Yusuf Haji's son, Abdul Yusuf, as the perfect man to fly its flag to the Upper House

The Jubilee Party has unveiled its candidates for the upcoming May 18 by-elections in Garissa county and Juja and Bonchari constituencies.

The ruling party settled on the former Garissa senator Yusuf Haji's son, Abdul Yusuf, as the perfect man to fly its flag back to the Upper House.

In Juja, the President Uhuru Kenyatta-led outfit will rely on the late area MP Francis Waititu's wife Susan in its bid to retain the seat which fell vacant on Monday, February 22.

In what could renew the bitter rivalry between the Jubilee and the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM), the former announced it would back one-time MP Zebedeo Opore for the Bonchari seat.

The ODM brigade picked the former embattled Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority director-general Pavel Oimeke who resigned amidst a corruption case against him.

He emerged as the favourite in the race that attracted three candidates, including immediate former Kisii county executive committee member in charge of water John Momanyi and businessman David Ogega.

Handshake under siege

The two parties enjoy a warm working relationship and have supported a single "handshake" candidate in previous by-elections.

The Jubilee vice chairperson David Murathe moved in to calm the growing disquiet among the two parties' supporters, maintaining the handshake was still intact.

The former Gatanga MP argued that since none of the two parties held the vacant Bonchari parliamentary seat, it was proper for each of them to fight for the seat.

"The fielding of candidates by ODM and Jubilee will not scuttle the handshake because none of the two parties held the seat. It is up for grabs," he stated on Monday, March 23.

Bonchari seat was declared vacant following death of MP Oroo Oyioka, who was elected on the People's Democratic Party whose party leader Omingo Magara said they would support the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) candidate.

The newly-formed Deputy President William Ruto-leaning UDA nominated Oyioka's widow Teresa Bitutu for the mini poll.
