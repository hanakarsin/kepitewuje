A 75-year-old man has returned to his ancestral home in Kolait village, Teso North, Busia county where he left 46 years ago to search for greener pastures.

Francis Indek Arachabon reportedly left in 1975 aged 29 to visit his uncle in Kimilili but he later extended his journey to Soy to look for employment where he broke contacts with his family.

According to Nation, his family unsuccessfully searched for him from his relatives, police stations and even mortuaries.

Surprisingly, Arachabon returned home on Sunday, January 17, and was received by his brother Erneo Okadapao aged 71 years.

Okadapao said he was surprised to see his brother whom he has spent a lot of resources searching for including seeking services of witchdoctors and religious leaders but all was in vain.

The septuagenarian added that a church leader from Uganda had promised him that his brother would return and it has materialised.

Started a family

Arachabon said for the years he has been away, he got married to Susan Tabolei and is blessed with three children.

He attributed the thoughts of not thinking to return home to the pleasant company he got from his new friends.

The elderly man added that he tilled people's land and did menial jobs for survival.

In a separate story, residents of Nabisiongo village in Matayos constituency, Busia County were shocked after a man they presumed dead and buried four months ago returned home.

The family of Steven Ogolla had conducted his burial after he went missing for four months.

Ogolla’s brother Steven Ouma fled when he saw his brother claiming he has seen a ghost as they had buried him.
