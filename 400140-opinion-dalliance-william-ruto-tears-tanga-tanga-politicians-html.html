Opinion: Dalliance with William Ruto will end in tears for many Tanga Tanga politicians

June 24, 2024
Post

All is not well in Deputy President William Ruto’s camp; nasty sectarian wars are threatening to scuttle his Tanga Tanga movement.

Power struggles and deep mistrust have rocked the camp even before the ink which the Registrar of Political Parties used to sign the change of name certificate that gave rise to United Democratic Alliance (UDA), formerly The Party for Development and Reforms (PDR), fully dries up.

This is after UDA, which is headed by billionaire businessman Johnstone Muthama, outmanoeuvred other Tanga Tanga politicians who had desperately rushed to form political outfits in the hope that the DP would either pick them or include them in his alliance ahead of 2022.

There were fears that Muthama, the former Machakos senator, had strategically fronted UDA as the vehicle Ruto will use for his presidential bid instead of putting all the parties under one umbrella.

The fears of the hopeful party owners were confirmed after the DP openly backed former assistant minister Margaret Wanjiru’s bid for the elusive Nairobi governor by-election on a UDA ticket.

Their misery was made complete when some key Ruto allies hinted that the outfit, which has a wheelbarrow as its symbol and “kazi ni kazi” as its slogan, will be his official 2022 political vehicle.

Those left embarrassingly clinging onto briefcase parties in their hands include Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria (People’s Empowerment Party), former Agriculture Cabinet Secretary Mwangi Kiunjuri (The Service Party), Malindi MP Aisha Jumwa (Kaddu Asili), Omingo Magara (People’s Democratic Party) and former Kenya Ports Authority boss Daniel Manduku, who owns the Party of Economic Development.

More ominously for Ruto, it is now clear that the politicians’ rush to form the outfits and then align them to his so-called Hustler movement is a clear indication that their sole interest is to use him for their own selfish interests.

They are not truly interested in helping the DP to achieve his ambitions. Indeed, there is no common ideology most of those in Tanga Tanga share that can lay a foundation for offering an alternative to the current political dispensation in the country.

That is why they are now disappointed that Muthama and others in UDA stole the show from them when they unveiled the wheelbarrow, the signature symbol of the Hustlers, as their party symbol.

Left alone, none of these Tanga Tanga MPs can politically survive as they are all underwhelming when ranged against formidable forces backing the Uhuru Kenyatta/Raila Odinga Handshake in their backyards.

Notably, Kuria, Jumwa and Kiunjuri are only clinging onto Ruto’s coat pockets after making costly political miscalculations. Jumwa and a section of pro-Ruto MPs, as well as former Mombasa senator Hassan Omar - who is desperate to get out of the political cold - are seeking to build a strong Coast-based party.

Even Muthama himself is fighting hard to remain relevant having lost the grip he had on Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka.

He only shifted to Ruto’s camp after realizing Kalonzo was determined to chart his own destiny by rallying behind the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

He does not want to spend another five years in the cold having stayed out of the 2017 contest in the hope of landing a plum job in a NASA government. But it will not be easy. Already, his attempts to handpick a UDA candidate for the Machakos senatorial by-election is threatening to split pro-Ruto MPs in Ukambani.

This and the failed attempt to trick independent candidates in Matungu to withdraw in support of a handpicked candidate, which has left an egg on the face of former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale, another desperate fellow, should send alarm bells ringing for those hoping to ride on a Ruto wave in 2022.

It goes to show that the forces behind UDA are playing their cards close to their chests with Ruto’s apparent blessings, and they should brace themselves for boardroom nominations in 2022.

No one will be assured of the party’s ticket and the sooner they realise this, the better. It will end in premium tears for many of them.

And even if Ruto hammers together some sort of alliance, unlike President Kenyatta who genuinely oversaw the collapsing of 12 political parties to form Jubilee Party and gave them a fair chance both at the nominations and subsequent appointments, the DP is known to have for his electoral authoritarianism as was the case when he messed up the 2017 primaries in key regions.

This was especially the case in Mt Kenya where strong candidates were rigged out as Ruto sought to impose those he saw as assets for his 2022 ambitions. The exercise was so chaotic that Kenyatta, as the party leader, had to step in and order repeat elections, though not all damage was undone.

When it comes to Ruto, there is no civility. Politicians allied to him are progressively learning this.

The writer is Milan Kiplagat, a regular commentator on social, economic and political affairs.
