Former Sheffield United attacker and Jamaica’s all-time leading scorer Luton Shelton has sadly passed away aged 35.

Shelton passed away after a lengthy battle with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, a motor neurone disease.

He was first diagnosed with the disease in 2018 when he had just retired from the game.

A number of clubs which he represented, including Sheffield Wednesday, have since come out with tributes towards him.

"Sheffield United is saddened to hear reports in the Caribbean indicating the passing of our former striker, Luton Shelton, aged just 35,” Sheffield said in a statement.

"The condolences of all at the Blades are with Luton's family at this difficult time."

The Blades also paid tribute to Shelton in their FA Cup fourth round tie against Plymouth on Saturday, January 23.

Another club which sent out condolences was Norwegian outfit Valerenga who wrote on Twitter, “The sad news (of) Luton Shelton's untimely passing has reached us.

Shelton passed away with the proud record of being Jamaica’s all time goal scorer with 35 goals from 75 international matches.

He made four appearances as a Sheffield United player between January 2007 to the summer of 2008 and one of his most memorable moments was when he scored a goal against Manchester City in a 2-1 FA Cup win.

His goal was quite bizarre as well as it deflected off a balloon that made its way to the pitch.

What is amyotrophic lateral sclerosis

It is a disease which mainly affects the muscles and impacts physical capabilities.

Known popularly as ALS, it mostly begins with weakness in a limb or slurred speech. progressive nervous system disease that affects nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord, causing loss of muscle control.

It is also known as the Lou Gehrig's disease, named after the baseball player who was diagnosed with it.

One of the biggest symptoms is twitching in the arms, shoulders and tongue, with constant muscle cramps and it mostly starts in the limbs as it slowly travels to the rest of the body.
