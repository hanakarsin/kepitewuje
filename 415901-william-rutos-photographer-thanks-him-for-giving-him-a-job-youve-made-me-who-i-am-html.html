Deputy President William Ruto's photographer has taken to his social media platforms to thank the DP for giving him an opportunity to work for him.

The photographer who previously worked in K24 and state house said he was a budding photographer with no hope of what life had for him but Ruto made him the man he is today.

In his heartwarming message on his Facebook on Thursday, June 17, the journalist identified as Mwangi thanked Ruto for not only the opportunity to work for him but for also trusting him and his colleagues.

"I was a village photographer with no hope, not sure of what life had for me, you have made me the man I am today. From our hearts sir, Dr William Samoei Ruto (WSR) The Deputy President Of Kenya, thank you very much for trusting in us and been so kind that you gave us an opportunity to serve with you. Myself, my colleagues," he said.

Mwangi previously worked in K24 and he rose through the ranks to become Ruto's photographer.

In a separate report, TUKO.co.ke reported that Ruto thanked a Dubai-based Kenyan banker who volunteered to come back to the country to campaign for his 2022 presidential bid.

The woman identified as Hellen took to her Twitter to profess her love for the deputy president and noted she will sacrifice to be Ruto's agent for free on the campaign trail.

Responding to her tweet, the DP thanked the lady, who according to her Twitter bio works as a credit officer in the Islamic Bank in Dubai.
