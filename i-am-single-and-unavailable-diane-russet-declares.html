Former housemate in the Big Brother Naija (BBNaija) reality TV show, Diane Russet has declared that she doesn’t want to be linked with any guy at the moment.

The reality star made the submission in response to a Twitter user who submitted that Diane and another Nigerian singer, Mayorkun would make a cute couple.

“I don’t know if they’ve met before, but Diane Russet and Mayorkun would make the cutest couple,” the fan had tweeted.

However, Diane in her response to the tweet, declared that she doesn’t want to be linked with any male friend or colleague in the entertainment industry.

She also cautioned her fans against attempting to matchmake her.

“I understand people care, but please, I don’t want to be shipped with any of my male friends or colleagues. I am single and unavailable.”

“Chapters that have been closed let it remain closed. Any tweet disguised as a joke I will block #respectfully Love and light always,” she wrote.

Meanwhile, Kanye West, the rapper-turned-entrepreneur, has reportedly become the richest black man in the United States after his net worth increased to a cumulative sum of $6.6 billion.

Kanye who is currently finalizing a mutual divorce from his estrange wife, Kim Kardashian is now worth $6.6 billion, a significant rise from his previous numbers, according to TheCable.

Recall that Forbes had reported in April 20202, that West is worth $1.3 billion after an analysis it conducted but West maintained the figure was much higher, saying: “It’s not a billion. It’s $3.3 billion since no one at Forbes knows how to count.”

But it is now understood that Yeezy, West’s sneaker and apparel business for which he had partnered with Adidas AG and Gap Inc., two fashion giants, is now valued between $3.2 billion and $4.7 billion by the UBS Group AG.

The value of the new Gap collaboration with Yeezy, which is scheduled to hit stores this summer, is now estimated to be “as much as $970 million of that total,” according to Bloomberg, after citing a bank estimate.

West, 43, also has $122 million in cash and stock and $110 million from his music catalogue, alongside $1.7 billion in other assets, including SKIMS, an underwear/loungewear business owned by Kim Kardashian, his estranged wife.
