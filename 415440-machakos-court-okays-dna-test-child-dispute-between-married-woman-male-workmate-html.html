Machakos Court Okays DNA Test of Child in Dispute Between Married Woman and Male Workmate

Post
  • Two workmates from the Kenya Bureau of Standards have been fighting over the paternity of a six-year-old child
  • The mother of the child, who is a married woman, had challenged a ruling that gave her male colleague the nod to conduct a DNA test
  • Justice Odunga dismissed her appeal and okayed the paternity test saying the man had reasonable grounds to ask for the DNA

A Machakos court has okayed a paternity test of a child who is at the centre of a dispute between a married woman and her fellow male colleague.

The court gave the man, identified anonymously as FP, to carry out the DNA test despite the mother's objection. The two are workmates at the Kenya Bureau of Standards.

According to a report by the Standard, the woman, identified anonymously as AN, to protect the identity of the child, had filed an appeal in the High Court against a court's ruling that had given FP the green light to carry on the paternity test.

Romantic relationship

In his argument, FP claimed the six-year-old child was born out of a relationship with AN which he said happened six years ago.

He filed the case in 2017 seeking to have access and provide for the minor as the father.

However, the woman opposed FP's application saying the child was a product of her marriage to her husband, listed as RM, adding that her workmate's intent was to break her marriage because she declined his sexual advances.

Justice George Odunga dismissed AN's appeal and ruled that a paternity test should be taken to solve the dispute.

Odunga maintained that no law ground stops FP from having the paternity test given he has reasonable grounds on why he says he is the child's father.

The KBS employees both produced different birth certificates of the child which showed different names of the father.

The woman's document showed her husband, RM, was the father to her child while her male colleague showed a birth certificate that bore his name as the father.

In a separate story, a man's 24-year union with his wife ended after DNA tests results showed that he only sired three of the four kids he raised with his wife.

The disgruntled man's wife admitted to him that the three children were fathered by her boss.

The man decided to conduct a DNA test to ascertain the paternity of the four children after he got suspicious of his wife's infidelity.

The wife had also disclosed to her second-born daughter that her real father was her boss after the damsel questioned why she and her other two sisters resemble the woman's boss.
