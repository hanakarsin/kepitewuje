Police storm ex-CS Rashid Echesa's home after IEBC called for his arrest

Police officers gave raided former Sports Cabinet Secretary Rashid Echesa's home hours after the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) chairman Wafula Chebukati called for his arrest.

The former CS was captured in a video on the morning of Thursday, March 4, assaulting an IEBC official in a polling station at Matungu constituency.

The law enforcers stormed his home in Shibale area in Mumias West on the evening of Thursday.

Echesa allegedly slapped Peter Okura at Bulonga Primary School polling station in Matungu constituency for prohibiting United Democratic Alliance (UDA) agent from accessing the station.

The IEBC official claimed the agent had refused to wear a face mask.

Speaking to the press on Thursday, Chebukati termed the incident as an apparent attack on the law and further urged police officers to nab the Tanga Tanga leader.

"We condemn the incident in Matungu where one of our officials was slapped by a former CS and we call on the police to apprehend the suspect," he said.

Though the various by-elections that took place all around the country were marred by instances of chaos and violence, Chebukati said all the mini-polls were peaceful.

"The exercise is peaceful all around the country apart from the small hitches that our police officers took care of," he said.

Besides Echesa, several other Ruto allies were also caught on the wrong side of the law during the various mini-polls.

At least four MPs allied to UDA were arrested and are being detained at Bungoma Police Station.

Didmus Barasa (Kimilili), Beson Koech (Belgut), Wilson Kogo (Mosop), Samson Cherargei (Nandi Senator) were arrested and locked up in the cells without clear charges being levelled against them.

Reports indicated the lawmakers were found with guns in their vehicles which were seen as a threat to voters.
