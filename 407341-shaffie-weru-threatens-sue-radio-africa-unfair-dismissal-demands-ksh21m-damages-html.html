Shaffie Weru Threatens to Sue Radio Africa for Unfair Dismissal, Demands KSh 21m in Damages

May 27, 2024
Post

- Shaffie Weru accused his former employer of unlawfully terminating his employment contract

- He said the company had not given him chance for fair hearing over the "insensitive remarks" that he allegedly made and which led to him getting the sack

- He now wants Radio Africa Group to pay him a total of KSh 21 million in damages

Ex-Homeboyz Radio presenter Shaffie Weru has threatened to file a suit against his former employer, the Radio Africa Group (RAG), over what he said was unlawful termination of his contract.

Via his lawyers, Shaffie who was the Programs Controller at the station served the RAG's general manager with a demand later in which he specified that he be paid a sum of KSh 21 million in damages.

The media personality further asked for his one month’s salary in lieu of notice which was KSh 682,500.

Also emphasised in the letter was his salary for 12 months which had been summed up to KSh 8,190,000, accounting for the "unfair dismissal" and compensation of KSh 12,285,000 as well.

Shaffie faulted his former employer for intimidation after they denied him the chance for a fair hearing, discriminating against him and further violating his constitutional rights by circulating his termination letter on social media.

The former Kiss 100 presenter through his lawyer claimed that he learnt of his dismissal on social media and that to date, he was still not aware of the "insensitive comments" that he allegedly made and which led to the termination of his employment.

"Your suspension letter dated March 26, 2021, referred to "inappropriate and insensitive comments" allegedly made on March 25, 2021, while your summary dismissal letter referred to "inappropriate and insensitive comments" made on March 24, 2021. Our client is still lost and confused as to the exact comments you allude to," read the demand letter in part.

Should Radio Africa fail to meet the aforementioned demands in the next seven days, the radio presenter via his lawyer will be instituting legal action against the company.

By the time of the termination, Shaffie had worked for Radio Africa Group for 18 years serving in different stations and positions within the company.

He was sacked alongside Dj Joe Mfalme and Neville Muysa over alleged derogatory remarks that were demeaning to women and which were made during their breakfast show dubbed the 'Lift Off' on Homeboyz Radio.

The trio was having a discussion on their show and in it, they incorporated the story of a lady by the name of Eunice who was sexually abused by her boyfriend following a disagreement.

Eunice had accused her boyfriend of pushing her from the 12th floor of a storey building after she turned down his sexual advances.

Weighing in on the matter, Shaffie with his colleagues shifted blame to the said woman, even accusing her of being "too loose" and "too available".

Their remarks irked a section of Kenyans who found their comments to be insensitive, demanding action against them.

Their employer bowed to pressure from netizens and sponsors, suspending them for two weeks before terminating their employment after the pressure became so unbearable.
