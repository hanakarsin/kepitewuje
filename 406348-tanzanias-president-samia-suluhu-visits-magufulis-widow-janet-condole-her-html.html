The newly sworn-in Tanzanian President Samia Suluhu Hassan has condoled with the bereaved family of former president John Pombe Magufuli.

Suluhu visited the family to deliver her government's condolence message to Magufuli's widow Janet on the afternoon of Friday, March 19.

The new head of state, who was accompanied by Tanzanian government Chief Secretary Bashiru Ally, signed the condolence book.

The 61-year-old politician was earlier on, sworn in as the country's sixth president a day after the passing on of her predecessor Magufuli.

She called for national unity of purpose so that the country could focus on the future without Magufuli.

“This is a time to bury our differences and be one as a nation. This is not a time for finger-pointing, but it is a time to hold hands and move forward together," she stressed.

The new president, dressed in red and black, then proceeded to inspect a guard of honour mounted by the Tanzania Defence Forces.

The swearing-in event was low-key, without other heads of state, but was graced by former presidents Jakaya Kikwete and Hassam Mwinyi.

The Speaker of the National assembly and other dignitaries were also present.

Suluhu became Tanzania's first female president, joining a short list of African women who hold the top political position.

The new president will then chair a Cabinet meeting, with the next task being choosing her vice president, who must be okayed by the ruling party Chama Cha Mapenduzi (CCM).

The Tanzanian constitution requires that the vice president takes over for the remaining term if the sitting president dies, resigns or is indisposed.

Suluhu was also the first woman to serve as Tanzanian vice president following the 2015 General Election when she ascended to power on a CCM party ticket alongside Magufuli.

They were both re-elected for a second term in 2020. Before her tenure as vice president, she served as Makunduchi MP from 2010 to 2015.

She was also the Minister of State in the Vice President’s Office for Union Affairs from 2010 to 2015.
