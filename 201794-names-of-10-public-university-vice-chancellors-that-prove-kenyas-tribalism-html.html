Moi University VC protest prove ethnic appointments

Post

- Politicians stormed Moi University after Education CS Fred Matiang’i appointed Laban Ayiro as the acting VC

- Governor Mandago said the appointment of Laban Ayiro was wrong because he is non-Kalenjin

- Several public universities are being headed by VC's from dominant ethnic tribes where university is located

The move made by two governors and politicians who led residents to storm Moi University protesting the appointment of the new Vice-Chancellor (VC) has raised eyebrows.

Uasin Gishu Governor Jackson Mandago and Elgeyo Marakwet's Alex Tolgos led politicians on Tuesday, September 20, demanding the installation of Isaac Kosgey as the VC.

According to them, Kosgey is Kalenjin and deserves the appointment compared to Laban Ayiro who is of a different ethnic tribe.

The protests against the appointment because Ayiro is a non-Kalenjin tribesman pointed to other VC appointments in several public universities.

Here is a list of Vice Chancellors in Kenya, located in different counties in Kenya.

1. Peter Mbithi-  University of Nairobi

2. Paul Wainaina-Kenyatta University

3. Julius Omondi Nyabundi- Maseno University

4. Fredrick A. Ochieng' Otieno - Masinde Muliro University

5. John Sorana Akama - Kisii University

6. Mohammed S. Rajab - Pwani University

7. Laban Ayiro -  Moi University

8.South Eastern University - Francis Mathooko

9. Jaramogi Oginga Odinga University - S.G Agong

10. Technical University of Mombasa - Laila Uweso Abubakar

Laban Ayiro took over the leadership at Moi University in a ceremony at the Eldoret university on Wednesday, September 21 and will serve in the acting capacity for three months.
