Kirinyaga Man Embroiled in Legal Tussle with Purity Ngirici's Husband, Claims His Life is In Danger

June 01, 2024
Post
  • Businessman Kushen Muchiri claimed he has been receiving death threats since he reported to the police a robbery with violence case involving Andrew Ngirici
  • He claimed Ngirici in the company of a group of men attacked him and deflated his car tyres
  • Muchiri said he had been asked to withdraw the case but refused

A businessman based in Kirinyaga claims his life is in danger after reporting to the police a robbery with violence case involving Purity Ngirici's husband.

According to Kushen Muchiri, a group of men believed to have been sent by Andrew Ngirici attacked him, deflating all of his car tyres.

Muchiri whose wife is a police officer claimed that Ngirici had contracted another local businessman to intimidate and send death threats to him over the case.

He also divulged that his woes took a toll on his wife, who was transferred from her station in Mwea.

"I have been intimidated, he is on record saying he will kill me. He keeps sending people to inform me I will be killed. I don't know what to do. I was asked to either withdraw a robbery with violence case involving Ngirici, he had accompanied more than 20 men who attacked me in Sifa Gardens and deflated my car tyres," said Muchiri."They said if don't withdraw the case then my wife would be transferred from her station in Mwea East. Two days later, they transferred my wife after we refused to withdraw the case," stated Muchiri.

According to Muchiri, Ngirici had been conniving with the local police to sabotage his course of finding justice.

Tussle with auctioneers

Last month, Purity Ngirici found herself in trouble after auctioneers stormed her Karen-based palatial home over a KSh 5 million debt.

The Kirinyaga county women representative had used a chopper owned by Flex Air Charters to campaign during the 2017 polls.

The NCS auctioneers raided the lawmaker's house after failing to service the debt owed to Flex Air Charters, where she hired a helicopter during the 2016 campaigns.

According to reports, the auctioneers carted away household goods worth millions of shillings to recover the debt.

"We have already executed the orders and recovered all the households. There was arbitration but the MP refused to corporate,” NCS spokesperson said.

The Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker confirmed the incident and said she had resolved the matter after depositing a KSh 5 million cheque.

"The auctioneers descended on my residence and took away all the households including a KSh 2 million television set. Because I wanted to save my items, I decided to deposit the cash with the court as I move to court for an appeal,” she disclosed.
