The National Security Social Fund (NSSF) is a Kenyan agency created in 1965 through an Act of Parliament Cap 258 of the Laws of Kenya. The agency collects, safekeeps, responsibly invests, and distributes employees' retirement funds in formal and informal sectors. When you register for this scheme, you get an NSSF number for identification and recordkeeping. Learn how to check your NSSF number online and via SMS today.

How to check your NSSF number online

How to check your NSSF number in person

If you want to know how to know your NSSF number, you can visit the nearest NSSF office or Huduma Centre. Carry your national ID card, passport, or other recognised identification document.

Once you get there, explain your situation to one of the tellers. Tell them you cannot access your account number or account details.

Provide the supporting documents to show that you are the account owner. You will be given the number instantly. You will also get an encoded NSSF PIN key that you will use to access the lost information.

NB: If you have wondered how to check your NSSF number using your ID, you should follow the guide above. Carry your ID to the nearest Huduma Branch or NSSF office for assistance.

NSSF registration requirements

Only registered members have an NSSF number in Kenya. Before you commence the registration process, you need to ensure that you have the documents listed below.

  • Your Kenyan national identity card or valid passport
  • An appointment letter from your employer. This applies to all employed persons
  • Your employer’s name and NSSF number
  • A valid copy of your Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) PIN

How to check your status online

There is an NSSF self-service portal that members can use for multiple services, including checking the status of their accounts. Below are the steps you should take to check your status.

  • Visit the official NSSF portal on your mobile phone, laptop, or another internet-enabled device.
  • Click Member Self-Service Resources under the Self-Service Resources menu.
  • Enter your credentials to access your account details. Once logged in, you will be able to access all your records and confirm if everything is updated.
  • If you have forgotten your login credentials, lick Recover forgotten/lost credentials. This is the last option under member self-service links. Enter the information required. If you do not have the requested details, you should visit the nearest NSSF or Huduma office for assistance.
  • Once you visit the office, you will receive an encoded NSSF PIN key that you will use to access the portal. Visit the portal on an internet-enabled device and click Member Self-Service Resources.
  • Type in the encoded NSSF PIN key (This is the number you were issued when you visited the office).
  • Follow the prompts to change your password.
  • Prove you are not a robot, then click Save.
  • Next, log into your account using your new password and username. You will see your account number and contribution status after logging in.

How to check your NSSF status via SMS

Follow the steps below to check your account status via SMS.

  • Type NSSF, your NSSF number, date of birth, and your full name on your mobile phone in CAPS, leaving a space between each detail.
  • Send the details to 6773.
  • You will receive a PIN in SMS format.
  • Next, type Bal and the PIN received, and send to 6773.
  • Your statement balance will be sent via SMS.

NB: If you wish to request your statement via email, dial *303# on your phone. Select Member Services, then Contribution Information, then Email Contribution Statement.

Members should also know that the NSSF mobile app is available on Google Play Store. You can use the mobile app to access multiple services, including checking your account status.
