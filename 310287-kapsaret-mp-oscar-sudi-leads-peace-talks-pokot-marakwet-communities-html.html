Kapsaret MP Oscar Sudi leads peace talks between Pokot, Marakwet communities

May 18, 2024
Post

- Sudi said he was concerned with the escalating cases of insecurity in the region

- The MP claimed he intervened after efforts to get assistance from Interior ministry proved futile

- He blamed Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i and Permanent Secretary Karanja Kibicho for engaging in politics rather than providing security

- Sigor MP Peter Lochakapong called on the government to beef up security in schools to guarantee learners and teachers' safety

Kapsaret MP Oscar Sudi has led other leaders from Pokot and Marakwet communities in a peace meeting to tame increasing cases of insecurity in the region.

Sudi claimed he took the initiative after efforts to reach out to top security agents in the country to intervene and restore calm hit a snag.

Speaking at the border point of West Pokot and Elgeyo Marakwet counties on Saturday, July 12, the Jubilee lawmaker slammed Interior CS Fred Matiang'i and his PS Karanja Kibicho for "engaging in politics" at the expense of enhancing security.

"There are many people who have been given jobs but they do not know what they are supposed to do. There are those who have been tasked to provide security but they are busy engaging in politics and that is why I decided to step in myself," he said

The vocal MP lamented that his attempt to raise the matter with Kibicho proved futile after the PS allegedly snubbed his calls and text messages.

" I have been looking for PS Kibicho for almost four months now. I have been calling him, sending text messages trying to share ideas on how we can solve this problem but he has been ignoring my efforts," claimed Sudi

Sigor MP Peter Lochakapong who was part of the peace meeting called on the government to beef up security in schools in volatile areas along the West Pokot, Turkana and Elgeyo Marakwet borders to retain leaners and teachers in school.

The lawmaker said cases of learners being attacked by bandits were escalating and it was high time the government intervened.

"Innocent learners are being attacked and killed, in March, we had a case where a pupil from Chesegon Primary school was gunned down by bandits a few meters from the school compound. Another one has recently been attacked, this should not be allowed to go on," said Lochakapong.
