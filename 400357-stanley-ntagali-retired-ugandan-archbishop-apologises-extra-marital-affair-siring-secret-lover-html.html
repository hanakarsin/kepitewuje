A retired Ugandan archbishop of the Anglican Church of Uganda suspended for having extramarital affair with a married woman has apologised to Ugandans and his family for his actions.

Stanley Ntagali said he was just a man with no exception of sinning while asking Ugandans and the Church of Uganda to walk the talk and forgive him.

Ntagali issued the apology in a statement shared on his official Twitter handle on Tuesday, Janauray 19, noting that he was not only asking for an apology from Ugandans but also repenting to God.

"I apologise to my family, the church of Uganda, the whole Christian fraternity and most importantly repent to the most high," wrote the besieged bishop."Nothing can be done to account for or take away this but the blood of Christ," he added.

A source intimated to TUKO.co.ke that the said woman was married according to the Anglican Church customs but later seduced the man of the cloth into sleeping with her, resulting into the birth of their child.

According to the source, the woman used the child who is said to be about two years old to extort money from the retired archbishop with the threat of exposing him should he fail to do so.

The source was, however, keen to note that politics had a hand in exposing the archbishop since he was known to be a key critic of President Yoweri Museveni's regime.

The church stated Ntagali would not be allowed to perform duties such as preaching, offering sacraments or represent the Church of Uganda in any way until further notice.

He further urged the church's over 13 million congregants in the landlocked country to join hands in prayers and repent amid the cheating allegations.

Ntagali served as eighth archbishop of the Church of Uganda and had handed over office to Kaziimba on March 1, 2020, after he attained the retirement age.

The priest was the bishop of Kampala and had earlier served as bishop of Masindi-Kitara diocese from 2004 to 2012.
