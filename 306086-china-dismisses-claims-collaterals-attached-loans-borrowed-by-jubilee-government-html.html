China dismisses claims there are collaterals attached to loans borrowed by Jubilee government

May 28, 2024
Post

- The new Chinese ambassador to Kenya, Wu Peng, said no assets will be seized or controlled by China in case of loan default

- The envoy further noted there were no collaterals attached to the loans borrowed by the Kenyan government since 2013

- Peng said the loans agreement between the two governments were in line with international best practices

- According to the ambassador, funding for Naivasha-Kisumu SGR was not on the agenda of Presidents Uhuru's trip to Beijing

The Chinese ambassador to Kenya, Wu Peng, on Tuesday, May 21, dismissed claims that China's policies are a trap to African countries owing to the projects initiated by the Asian country in the continent.

Peng was speaking during a Press Club luncheon organised by the Kenya Editors Guild when he said there were no collaterals attached to the loans secured by the Jubilee administration.

He also noted the loans agreement between the Kenyan and Chinese governments were in line with common international best practices, the Standard reported.

“No country has been trapped in a debt crisis due to its cooperation with China. There are no secret clauses in the deals with Kenya,” he said.“I can assure you that any loan agreements between China and Kenya are in line with common international best practice," Peng added.

The new ambassador also noted that even in the event that a loan is defaulted by any government, no assets will be seized or controlled by the Asian nation.

"None of Kenya’s national assets has been mortgaged for the SGR loan, neither will any asset be seized nor controlled by China, even in a situation of default," he said. “I have personally engaged the companies and banks in Beijing and there is nothing to that effect," the envoy added.

He explained he would not make public the contracts between the two countries as he was bound by the rules of his country, noting every nation has its legal procedure to obtain information.

This echoed State House's Chief of Staff Nzioka Waita's remarks who affirmed that the same would not be provided from the Kenyan side.

“As much as I support transparency, there are obstacles to the contracts being made public. We have state-owned banks and company banks. There are the commercial interests which are protected,” said Peng

The envoy further affirmed that funding for Naivasha-Kisumu Standard Guarge Railway (SGR) was not on the agenda of Presidents Uhuru Kenyatta and his Chinese counterpart Xi Jinping's meeting in Beijing.

Peng said the Kenyan government was now focusing on the operations of the Mombasa-Nairobi SGR to maximise its effects.

“China fully respects Kenya's decision on when to start Naivasha-Kisumu SGR line. Indeed, Rome was not built in a day. China supports Kenya’s efforts to improve its infrastructural connectivity, which is key to economic development," he said.

He argued that huge infrastructural projects like SGR may take long to yield returns, but they are solid and valid assets.

“China attaches high importance to debt sustainability. Before making decisions, Chinese companies and banks, even the third party, go through rigorous feasibility studies, and review of a country’s credit rating," Peng said.
