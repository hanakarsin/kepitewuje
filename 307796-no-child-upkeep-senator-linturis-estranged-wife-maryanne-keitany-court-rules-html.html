No child upkeep for Senator Linturi's estranged wife Maryanne Keitany, court rules

July 21, 2024
Post

- Keitany had filed a case asking the court to compel Linturi to pay for her children upkeep

- The senator said the children were sired from Keitany's previous marriage

- The court ruled the senator had no parental responsibility for the minors

Maryanne Keitany, the estranged wife of Senator Mithika Linturi has suffered a blow after a children's court dismissed a case in which she wanted the legislator compelled to pay upkeep for her three children she got from a previous marriage.

In the matter which was before a Milimani law court, Keitany wanted Linturi to bear parental responsibility even though he was not the biological father of the children.

Senior Resident Magistrate G.M Gitonga agreed with the senator and dismissed the case saying it was an "abuse of the court process".

According to Linturi's lawyer, Muthomi Thiankolu, Keitany got maintenance orders for the children from John Langat who is the biological father of her two children in a divorce petition 150 of 2010.

"I am not the father of the children. I have never accepted or embraced the applicant’s children as my biological children as alleged in the main application and the supporting affidavit or at all," the senator argued.

The legislator through his advocate accused Keitany of using the case to scandalise him.

"The application is part of the applicant’s scheme to persecute, vex, scandalise, oppress and harass me for refusing to marry or enter into any form of marital relationship with her," he stated.

The court, however, pointed out that a person who is not a biological parent could acquire parental responsibility as contemplated by section 94 of the Children's Act and held Keitany could organise her pleadings and still sue Linturi.

The two have been embroiled in a vicious legal battle over their troubled union and property.
