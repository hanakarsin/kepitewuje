Start a Business

Starting a business is similar to boarding a matatu. You need to know where you are going, how much you require reaching your destination, what you will carry and the opportunities and challenges you will face on the way. The first step is to identify the type of business you want. You can always start by pinpointing any community needs in your area.

Just as 28-year-old Lorna Rutto recognized the need for better ways to dispose of plastics. Through her company EcoPost, Lorna creates eco-friendly fencing posts from recycled plastics. Lorna left a comfortable job at a local bank to venture into a business that many perceive as dirty. Today, Lorna is worth millions plus she employs numerous people directly and indirectly and contributes to environmental conservation.

If you do not know where to start, some business ideas Kenya that you can consider are restaurant/hotel, selling second-hand clothes, waste management, real estate development or management, car dealership, cereal, and consulting services. You can also become rich with no money legally by offering services. These may include house cleaning, catering, bookkeeping, website management among others.

Venture into Agriculture

Agriculture is a primary economic activity in Kenya. However, in Kenya, the demand for food exceeds the production of food. You can take advantage of this deficit to join other agriculture millionaires in Kenya like Martha Otieno. Homabay-based Martha ventured into Agriculture after trying her luck in the job market to no avail. Initially, she partnered with local farmers in the area to lease and cultivate the land. Over time, Martha was able to acquire 4 acres of land in the area.

Today, she rotates pepper, groundnuts, watermelon, and maize on the land. Martha also rears fish. The local community and the towns around are Martha’s immediate customers. She also exports some of her produce to Uganda. You can read more about Martha story from her interview with Citizen TV. Like Martha, you do not need to own a piece land. You can lease land for about Kshs. 6000 an acre though the price may vary depending on the demand and the area.
