Newsman Eric Njoka is marking almost a year since he was fired from K24 TV.

The news anchor reflected on losing his job via a Twitter message on May 6.

Eric recounted how his position was declared redundant by his former employer, saying one should let go of the past.

He also revealed that he would be starting his new role with India’s Zee Media Corporation Limited this weekend.

“It's almost a year since I was declared null n' void by my former employer, and as they say to prosper, you must let go of the past and all your detractors.

Shortly before his K24 exit, Eric was forced to apologise to the company after publicly venting about not getting paid.

He called out Media Max Limited, which runs K24 TV, disclosing that he had not received his dues for two months.

According to the news anchor, the company was frustrating many employees and their families, and he was not ready to bear the burden for not speaking against the issue.

Via Instagram Live, the journalist expressed regret over comments he made about the CEO and the company but was not sorry for speaking the truth.
